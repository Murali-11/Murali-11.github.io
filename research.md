## Research Associate — University of Bristol (2024–Present)

Research in Monitoring, Reporting and Verification (MRV) of greenhouse gas removal (GGR) technologies as part of the UKRI-funded **CO2RE** and EU Horizon-funded **PathFinder** projects.

- Leading development of research papers on MRV protocols for the full spectrum of GGR technologies, with emphasis on assessing durability, additionality and risks.
- Developing a common reporting framework for UNFCCC national inventory submissions for forest land reporting that allows for harmonisation, timeliness and consistency.
- Conducting Life Cycle Analysis of GGR technologies to assess measuring and monitoring techniques across projects and scientific literature.

## Research Intern — European Commission Joint Research Centre (2024)

- Conducted spatial analysis of forest clear-cut operations across the Czech Republic (2019–2021) by integrating two Pan-European remote sensing datasets: the PathFinder Forest Structure Maps and the European Forest Disturbance Atlas.
- Conducted multi-scenario statistical analysis to assess data quality impacts and identified detection bias in satellite-based harvest monitoring, demonstrating greater reliability in high-biomass forest stands.
- Provided biomass distribution analysis that directly informed parameterisation of the JRC carbon budget model, contributing empirical data on pre-harvest carbon stocks for improved forest carbon accounting and LULUCF reporting.

## Additional Research & Consultancy

**Geospatial Analyst (Part-time Consultancy) — Avon Needs Trees (2024–2025)**

- Developed a machine learning-based habitat classification system using Sentinel satellite data to map and analyse 800 hectares of landscape in the Lower Chew Valley, informing DEFRA's national Landscape Recovery scheme.
- Built models for assessing biodiversity, carbon stock changes and flood management using open-source data.
- Designed interactive web-based visualisation tools integrating habitat and ecosystem status maps to provide overview and tracking of landscape status for stakeholders and project managers.

**Geospatial Data Analyst — Tree Aid (2024)**

- Remote sensing and GIS analysis of vegetation cover and land classification to aid monitoring of carbon certification projects.
- Cleaning, loading and transforming large datasets covering multiple projects to analyse KPIs such as trees planted, survival rate and income levels.
- Presenting reports and analysis for carbon credit verification and validation with project auditors and creditors.
- Building monitoring and reporting dashboards to aid executives, policymakers and project teams with current progress on indicators and KPIs.
